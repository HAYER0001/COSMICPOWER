> **Quick Answer:** Makhana destroys chips in every nutritional metric. 50g chips: ~270 cal, 17g fat. 50g makhana: ~170 cal, <0.1g fat. Makhana has protein, fibre, and antioxidants — chips have almost none. Taste-wise, flavoured makhana (Creamy Cheese, Spicy Masala, Tangy Tomato) delivers the same savoury satisfaction without the oil slick or artificial aftertaste.

## The Fat Story

A single 50g bag of chips contains more fat than two tablespoons of cooking oil. That fat is absorbed during frying at high temperatures, which can create trans fats and acrylamide (a potential carcinogen formed when starchy foods are fried). Makhana is slow-roasted, not fried. The fat content is negligible.

## The Satiety Factor

Here is the most important metric that no nutrition label shows: how full you feel after eating. Makhana's protein + fibre combination triggers satiety hormones. Chips — being mostly fat and simple carbs — give you a dopamine hit but no fullness signal. You finish the bag and immediately want another.

This is why a 50g pack of makhana can satisfy your snack craving, while a 50g pack of chips often leads to reaching for a second bag.

These are not chips alternatives — they are a category upgrade.
