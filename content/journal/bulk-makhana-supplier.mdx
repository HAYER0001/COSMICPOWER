## Why Supplier Selection Matters

If you are building a brand around makhana — whether as a roasted snack brand, a HoReCa supplier, or an exporter — your raw material quality will define your finished product. Inconsistent grade leads to uneven roasting, poor pop yield, and unhappy customers.

## The Bulk Procurement Checklist

### 1. Grade Consistency

Not all makhana is equal. The trade grades by "suta":

| Grade | Size | Best For |
|-------|------|----------|
| 5–6 Suta (Jumbo) | 18–24mm | Premium retail, export, white-label roasting |
| 4–5 Suta (Standard) | 14–18mm | HoReCa, processed snacks, culinary |

Always ask for grade certification and request samples before committing to a full lot.

### 2. Sourcing Transparency

Where does the makhana come from? Direct farm sourcing means fewer middlemen, better prices, and traceability. Golden Deer sources directly from farming cooperatives in Bihar's Gangetic wetland belt — each lot is traceable to the grower cooperative and harvest date.

### 3. Sorting Standards

Insist on hand-sorted makhana with less than 2% broken pieces. Mechanical sorting misses discoloured seeds and irregular sizes that will ruin your roast consistency. Golden Deer's sorting team has decades of combined experience and manually inspects each batch.

### 4. Packaging

Standard bulk packaging options include: 250g retail pouches, 5kg bulk bags, 10kg bulk bags, and 25kg bulk bags. For extended shelf life, choose nitrogen-flushed packaging. Without nitrogen flushing, raw makhana has a shelf life of about 3 months; with nitrogen flushing, it extends to 8–12 months.

### 5. Lab Testing

A reputable supplier should provide third-party lab analysis covering:
- Moisture content (target: <10%)
- Broken pieces percentage
- Foreign matter
- Microbiological parameters
- Heavy metals (for export compliance)

### 6. MOQ Flexibility

The best suppliers work with businesses of all sizes. Golden Deer accommodates small-batch artisans (250g pouches for sampling) and large-scale exporters (multi-tonne container loads). MOQ is discussed on inquiry — not arbitrarily set.
